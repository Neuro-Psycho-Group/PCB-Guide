Parts on the Board

Everything that gets soldered to a PCB: the two mounting families, all common component types, and how to read package codes.

The two families

Through-hole vs Surface Mount

Every component you place on a PCB belongs to one of two mounting families. Understanding the difference is fundamental — it affects your tool choices, design rules, and soldering technique.

Your friend's tweezers

Surface mount soldering is absolutely manageable — but it requires practice, good light, and the right tools. Start with 0805-size components (1mm × 0.5mm), which are just large enough to pick up reliably. Avoid 0402 (0.6mm × 0.3mm) and anything smaller until you're comfortable.

Component types

Common PCB Components

Resistor

Limits current flow. Defined by its resistance value in Ohms (Ω). Can be used to set biasing voltages, limit LED current, form voltage dividers, and set RC time constants.

Schematic symbol: zig-zag (US) or rectangle (EU/IEC) · Code: R or RN

key specs
Value range1Ω to 10MΩ, E24/E96 series
Power rating1/8W to 1W typical
Tolerance1% (metal film) or 5% (carbon film)

Capacitor

Stores electrical charge. Used for power supply filtering, decoupling (removing noise near ICs), timing circuits, and coupling AC signals while blocking DC.

Types: ceramic (MLCC), electrolytic, tantalum, film · Code: C

key specs
Value1pF to 10,000µF
Voltage ratingMust exceed supply voltage with margin
PolarityElectrolytics/tantalums: observe +/−

Inductor

Stores energy in a magnetic field. Resists changes in current. Used in power converters (buck/boost regulators), RF filters, and impedance matching. Often paired with capacitors to form LC filters.

Types: air core, ferrite core, toroidal · Code: L

key specs
Value1nH to several Henries
DCRDC winding resistance (lower = better)
Saturation currentMax before inductance drops

Crystal / Oscillator

Provides a precise timing reference for microcontrollers and communication circuits. A crystal resonates at a specific frequency when electrical energy is applied (piezoelectric effect). An oscillator packages the crystal with its drive circuit.

Common frequencies: 8MHz, 12MHz, 16MHz, 32.768kHz (RTC) · Code: Y or XTAL

key specs
FrequencyMust match microcontroller spec
Load capacitanceDatasheet specifies Cload

Diode

Allows current to flow in one direction only. The cathode end (marked with a band) is the negative terminal. Used for reverse polarity protection, rectification (AC→DC), and signal clipping.

Types: rectifier, Schottky (fast/low dropout), Zener (voltage regulation), TVS (ESD protection) · Code: D

Forward voltage (Vf)~0.6V silicon, ~0.2V Schottky
PolarityBand = cathode (negative)

LED

Light Emitting Diode — a diode that emits light when forward-biased. Always requires a current-limiting resistor in series; without one, it will draw unlimited current and burn out immediately.

Resistor calculation: R = (Vsupply − Vf) / If · Code: D or LED

Forward voltage (Vf)1.8V red · 2.0V yellow · 3.0V blue/white
Typical forward current10–20mA
PolarityAnode (+) to positive supply

Transistor (BJT)

A semiconductor device used for switching and amplification. A small current into the base controls a larger current through the collector-emitter path. NPN and PNP are the two polarities.

Uses: logic-level switching, motor drivers, signal amplification · Code: Q or T

TerminalsBase, Collector, Emitter
hFE (gain)Current gain: Ic / Ib
Vce(sat)Saturation voltage — low is good

MOSFET

Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor Field Effect Transistor. Like a BJT but voltage-controlled rather than current-controlled. The gate voltage controls the drain-source current. Far more efficient for power switching than BJTs.

Uses: motor control, power switching, DC-DC conversion · Code: Q

TerminalsGate, Drain, Source
Vgs(th)Gate threshold voltage to turn on
Rds(on)On-state resistance — lower = less heat

Microcontroller (MCU)

A complete computer on a single chip: CPU, memory (flash + RAM), and peripherals (GPIO, UART, SPI, I²C, ADC, PWM). The brains of most embedded devices. Programmed in C/C++ or MicroPython.

Popular choices: ATmega328P (Arduino), STM32 series, RP2040 (Raspberry Pi Pico), ESP32 (WiFi+BT) · Code: U

Supply voltageCheck datasheet — often 3.3V or 5V
Decoupling caps100nF on every power pin

Voltage Regulator

Converts an unregulated input voltage to a stable output. Linear regulators (LDO) are simple but inefficient — excess voltage is wasted as heat. Switching regulators (buck/boost/buck-boost) are efficient but more complex to design.

Common: AMS1117-3.3 (LDO linear), LM7805, TPS6300x (switching) · Code: U or VR

LDO efficiencyVout/Vin — can be very low
Switching efficiency85–96% typical

Op-Amp

Operational Amplifier — a high-gain differential amplifier with two inputs (inverting −, non-inverting +) and one output. With external resistors, can be configured as an amplifier, comparator, filter, adder, or integrator.

Popular: LM358, LM741, TL072, AD8232 · Code: U

Open loop gainVery high (100,000+)
Closed loopSet by feedback resistors

Memory ICs

Dedicated memory devices for storing data beyond what fits in a microcontroller's internal flash. EEPROM for small config data (survives power loss, byte-writable). Flash for larger firmware or data storage.

Interfaces: SPI, I²C, parallel · Common: 24LC series (EEPROM), W25Q series (flash) · Code: U

Pin Headers & Sockets

The most common way to connect boards to other boards, cables, or components. 2.54mm (0.1") pitch is the standard for through-hole; various smaller pitches for SMD. Male headers have pins, female sockets accept pins.

Pitch: 2.54mm standard, 2.0mm, 1.27mm, 1.0mm · Code: J or P

Current per pin1A typical for 2.54mm

USB Connectors

For data communication and/or power. Micro-USB was the historical standard; USB-C is now dominant. Mechanically, USB-C is more durable. Electrically, USB-C can support faster data and higher power delivery.

Types: USB-C, Micro-USB, Mini-USB, Type-A · Code: J

USB 2.0 dataD+ / D− differential pair
Power5V VBUS + GND minimum

Test Points

Small exposed copper pads (or through-holes) placed on key signals specifically for probing during testing and debugging. Best practice is to place test points on every important net — power rails, clock lines, UART TX/RX.

Types: bare pad, through-hole pad, plated half-hole · Code: TP

⚑ Critical for debugging. Add them generously.

Fuse / Protection

Self-resetting polyfuses (PTCs) protect against overcurrent — they increase resistance dramatically when too much current flows, then reset when cooled. Transient Voltage Suppressors (TVS diodes) clamp voltage spikes protecting against ESD.

PTC: RXEF series · TVS: SMBJ, 1.5KE series · Code: F (fuse), D (TVS)

Decoding components

Reading Component Markings

Components are labelled in ways that aren't immediately obvious. Here's how to decode them — a skill you'll use every time you open a bag of parts.

Through-hole resistors are marked with coloured bands. Each colour represents a digit, a multiplier, or a tolerance. Four-band and five-band resistors are the most common.

4-band resistor: two value digits + multiplier + tolerance

5-band resistors (1% precision)

Precision metal film resistors use 5 bands: three value digits, a multiplier, and a tolerance band. Read the same way — e.g. Brown·Black·Black·Red·Brown = 1·0·0 × 100 = 10,000Ω = 10kΩ ±1%. The tolerance band is usually silver (±10%), gold (±5%), or a colour (±1% or better) and sits slightly separate from the value bands. Always check the direction — start from the end closest to the first band.

SMD resistors are too small for colour bands. Instead they carry a printed numeric code — either a 3-digit EIA code, a 4-digit code, or an EIA-96 alphanumeric code. Capacitors and inductors have their own systems too.

3-digit SMD code (standard)

Format: ABX where AB is the significant digits and X is the number of zeros to append.

4-digit SMD code (precision, 1%)

Same system but three significant digits: ABCX where ABC are the digits and X is the multiplier. Example: 4702 = 4·7·0 × 10² = 47,000Ω = 47kΩ.

EIA-96 alphanumeric code

Used on tiny 0402 and smaller resistors where even 3-digit codes won't fit. Consists of a two-digit number (from an EIA-96 table) followed by a letter (the multiplier). Example: 01A = 100Ω, 68C = 499kΩ. Look up the EIA-96 table — it's freely available online and in most component datasheets.

Capacitors have their own marking systems that differ by type. The key thing to remember: capacitor codes are usually in picofarads (pF), not Ohms.

Ceramic capacitor codes (SMD and disc)

Uses the same 3-digit system as SMD resistors, but the value is in picofarads. Example: 104 = 1·0 × 10⁴ pF = 100,000 pF = 100 nF = 0.1 µF (the classic decoupling capacitor).

Electrolytic capacitor markings

Electrolytics are large enough to print directly: value in µF, voltage rating, and a polarity marking. The negative lead is marked — look for a stripe with minus signs down the side of the can, or a shorter lead. The positive lead is longer. Always orient correctly: reverse polarity destroys an electrolytic capacitor, sometimes violently.

Voltage rating is a maximum, not a target

A capacitor marked 10µF 16V can be used in any circuit where the voltage across it stays below 16V. It won't "run" at 16V — that's just the maximum it can safely see. For reliability, always choose a capacitor with a voltage rating at least 2× your actual operating voltage. A 5V power rail should have capacitors rated at 10V or 16V minimum.

Integrated circuits carry a date/lot code that tells you when and where they were manufactured. This matters for tracking batch defects, checking for counterfeit parts, and understanding component age.

Standard date code format

Most ICs use a YYWW format on their marking: a two-digit year followed by a two-digit week number. Example: 2342 = year 2023, week 42 (mid-October 2023). This is usually one of several lines of text on the IC body.

Reading an IC marking

A typical IC marking has three or four lines:

Counterfeit ICs are common on eBay / AliExpress

Counterfeit ICs are a real problem — remarked chips (old chips relabelled as newer, higher-spec parts), clones, and outright fakes. Buy from authorised distributors (Mouser, Farnell, Digi-Key, RS) for any critical IC. Suspiciously cheap microcontrollers from third-party Amazon/eBay sellers are often counterfeit. The date code and lot number can sometimes reveal anomalies — codes inconsistent with a claimed genuine part.

IC Packages

Understanding IC Package Types

Integrated circuits come in many physical packages. The package determines the footprint you need in your PCB design and the soldering method required.

DIP / PDIP

Dual Inline Package. Two rows of through-hole pins. The classic hobbyist IC — fits breadboards, easy to solder, easy to socket.

Pitch: 2.54mm · Pin count: 8–40

SOIC-8/16

Small Outline IC. SMD version of DIP. Two rows of gull-wing leads. Very solderable by hand.

Pitch: 1.27mm · Common: op-amps, drivers

SOT-23

Small Outline Transistor. 3–6 pin SMD package for transistors, MOSFETs, regulators.

Body: 2.9 × 1.3mm · Solderable by hand

QFP / TQFP

Quad Flat Package. Four sides of gull-wing leads. Common for microcontrollers. Requires hot air or careful iron work.

Pitch: 0.5–0.8mm · Pins: 32–256

QFN / LQFPs

No leads — pads on the underside rim only, plus a thermal pad below. Compact. Requires hot air or reflow oven.

Pitch: 0.4–0.65mm · Thermally excellent

BGA

Ball Grid Array. Balls of solder underneath the chip in a grid. Cannot be hand-soldered. Requires reflow. Used in processors, FPGAs, memory.

Pitch: 0.4–1.0mm · Expert only

TO-220 / TO-92

Through-hole power device packages. TO-220 is the classic 3-pin package with a metal tab for heatsinking (voltage regulators, power MOSFETs).

Hole pitch: 2.54mm · Power rated

SOP / SSOP

Small Outline Package variants. SSOP is shrunk version. Narrower than SOIC. Common for older comms ICs and ADCs.

Pitch: 0.635–1.27mm · Careful iron work
