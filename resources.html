Books worth owning

Reading List

A short, opinionated list. Each book earns its place.

📗

The Art of Electronics Essential

Horowitz & Hill · 3rd Edition · Cambridge University Press

The definitive reference for analogue and digital electronics. Not a PCB design book specifically, but understanding the circuits you're building makes your layout decisions ten times better. Covers everything from op-amps to transistors to microprocessors. Buy it. It never goes out of date.

📘

Signal and Power Integrity — Simplified Highly recommended

Eric Bogatin · 3rd Edition · Pearson

The clearest introduction to signal integrity, transmission lines, and power delivery networks in print. Written for practising engineers, not academics. If your boards operate above 50MHz or use USB/Ethernet/PCIe, this is essential reading. Bogatin explains complex concepts with physical intuition rather than mathematics.

📙

PCB Design for Real-World EMI Control

Bruce Archambeault · Kluwer Academic Publishers

The best practical guide to designing boards that pass EMC testing first time. Covers ground planes, decoupling, filtering, and layout techniques with measured results showing what actually works. More practical and less mathematical than most EMC texts.

📕

High Speed Signal Propagation

Howard Johnson & Martin Graham · Prentice Hall

The companion to their classic "High Speed Digital Design". Goes deeper on transmission line theory and board stackup effects. Reference material for 4+ layer board design with DDR memory, PCIe, or SERDES interfaces. Not beginner material — read Bogatin first.

📓

KiCad Like a Pro

Peter Dalmaris · Tech Explorations

The best step-by-step KiCad tutorial in book form. Takes you from installation through a complete board design. Available as a book or online course. Recommended if you prefer structured written instruction over YouTube tutorials.

techexplorations.com ↗

Video learning

YouTube Channels

Phil's Lab Essential for beginners

Phillip Salmony · Electronics Engineer · ~500k subscribers

The single best channel for learning PCB design with KiCad. Phil designs real professional boards on camera — STM32 projects, audio DSP boards, RF designs — and explains every decision as he makes it. His "STM32 PCB Design" series is the perfect companion to your first real board. Also covers signal integrity, SPICE simulation, and embedded firmware.

youtube.com/@PhilsLab ↗

Robert Feranec

Robert Feranec · Professional PCB Designer · ~250k subscribers

Deep dives into professional PCB design — layer stackups, high-speed routing, design reviews of real commercial products. His "How to Design PCB Layout" series is excellent. More intermediate/advanced than Phil's Lab but very practical. Also does design reviews of viewer-submitted boards, which are educational in themselves.

youtube.com/@RobertFeranec ↗

EEVblog

Dave Jones · Electronic Engineer · ~900k subscribers

The longest-running electronics engineering YouTube channel. Teardowns, design reviews, multimeter reviews, and educational "EEVblog 101" fundamentals. Less PCB-design-specific, but invaluable for building general electronics intuition. Dave's no-nonsense approach and willingness to show failures makes the channel uniquely valuable.

youtube.com/@EEVblog ↗

Altium Academy

Altium · Official channel

Despite being Altium's marketing channel, much of the content is software-agnostic and genuinely educational. Their "PCB Design Principles" series covers controlled impedance, stackup design, and differential pairs well. Worth watching for concept explanations even if you use KiCad.

youtube.com/@AltiumAcademy ↗

Online Communities

Where to buy components

UK & European Component Suppliers

Component availability and pricing varies significantly between distributors. Check multiple sources — the same chip can vary 5× in price between distributors.

RS Components

UK distributor · same-day dispatch

The go-to for UK professional and hobbyist orders. Huge stock, fast delivery (next day on most items), reliable. More expensive than LCSC for small quantities but you get it tomorrow. Good for urgent components and anything not on LCSC.

uk.rs-online.com ↗

Farnell

UK distributor · same-day dispatch

RS Components' main UK competitor. Similar range and pricing. Worth checking both RS and Farnell — each has exclusives and sometimes significantly different pricing on the same part. Element14 is their international brand.

uk.farnell.com ↗

Mouser

US distributor · worldwide shipping

Enormous selection, often the best option for newer or niche components not stocked in the UK. Ships from the US but usually arrives within a week. No minimum order. Excellent part search and parametric filtering.

mouser.co.uk ↗

Digi-Key

US distributor · worldwide shipping

Similar to Mouser — vast selection, ships from Minnesota, USA. Often has better pricing than Mouser on certain product lines. UK site avoids customs on most orders. Their parametric search is particularly powerful.

digikey.co.uk ↗

LCSC

Chinese distributor · JLCPCB sister company

The cheapest source for SMD passives and common ICs. Ships from China (1–3 weeks). Tightly integrated with JLCPCB's PCBA service — their part numbers map directly into JLCPCB's component library. Best for bulk orders of standard parts.

lcsc.com ↗

Pimoroni / The Pi Hut

UK hobbyist suppliers

UK-based hobbyist suppliers with good stock of development boards, modules, sensors, and accessories. Fast UK delivery. Not for raw components — but excellent for Raspberry Pi, breakout boards, and development kits. Pimoroni also sells their own excellent hardware designs.

pimoroni.com ↗

Your toolkit

Free Tools for PCB Designers

KiCad Free

EDA / PCB Design

The full professional EDA suite — schematic editor, PCB layout editor, 3D viewer, and Gerber viewer. The tool to learn first and the tool most hobbyist designers use professionally.

kicad.org ↗

EasyEDA Free

Web-based EDA

Browser-based EDA with massive component library. Best for absolute beginners — zero setup, instant access. Direct ordering to JLCPCB from within the tool.

easyeda.com ↗

LTspice Free

Circuit Simulation (SPICE)

Simulate circuits before building them. Check your power supply's transient response, verify filter roll-off frequencies, or test your LED resistor calculation. Made by Analog Devices, free forever.

analog.com/ltspice ↗

Saturn PCB Toolkit Free

PCB Calculators

A collection of PCB design calculators: trace width for current capacity, via current, microstrip impedance, differential pair spacing, thermal resistance. Essential reference tool. Windows application, also available as a web version.

saturnpcb.com ↗

JLCPCB Gerber Viewer Free

Gerber Verification

Upload your Gerber zip and view every layer before ordering. The best free online Gerber viewer — cleaner and more reliable than most desktop alternatives. Always check your files here before submitting an order.

jlcpcb.com ↗

Component Search Engine Free

Part Sourcing

Searches Digi-Key, Mouser, Farnell, RS, LCSC, and others simultaneously. Find the cheapest or fastest-available option for any component across all major distributors in one search.

componentsearchengine.com ↗

Octopart Free

Part Search & Comparison

Multi-distributor component search engine. Great for comparing prices and availability across distributors, and finding datasheet links. Owned by Altium but free to use.

octopart.com ↗

SnapEDA / Ultra Librarian Free

Component Libraries

Free libraries of schematic symbols and PCB footprints for thousands of components, downloadable in KiCad format. Essential when a component isn't in KiCad's built-in library. Always verify downloaded footprints against the datasheet.

snapeda.com ↗

Falstad Circuit Simulator Free · Browser

Interactive Circuit Simulator

A browser-based circuit simulator with animated current flow. Excellent for quickly visualising how a circuit works. Not as rigorous as SPICE but much faster to set up. Great for understanding new circuits before implementing them.

falstad.com/circuit ↗

KiCad Footprint Generator Free

Footprint Creation

KiCad's built-in "Footprint Wizard" and the community KiCad Footprint Generator scripts on GitHub can automatically generate footprints for ICs from their datasheet parameters. Useful for unusual packages not in the standard library.

kicad.org/libraries ↗

Putting it together

A Structured Learning Path

There's no single right order, but this progression takes you from absolute beginner to confident designer in the most efficient way.

Month 1 · Zero to first board

Install KiCad, design, and order one board

  • Work through this site cover to cover: History → Anatomy → Components → Workflow
  • Watch Phil's Lab "KiCad 6 STM32 Hardware Design" series on YouTube
  • Design the LED blinker from the First Board tutorial
  • Order from JLCPCB, solder it, make it work

Months 2–3 · Build confidence

Progressively more complex boards

  • Rebuild the blinker using SMD 0805 components — same circuit, new skills
  • Design a simple breakout board for a microcontroller you use (RP2040, STM32, ESP32)
  • Post your layouts to r/PrintedCircuitBoard for review before ordering
  • Read Horowitz & Hill Chapter 1–5 (passive components, amplifiers, logic)

Months 4–6 · Intermediate skills

Power circuits and communication interfaces

  • Design a board with a switching regulator (LM2596, TPS6300x)
  • Add USB-C — implement the differential pair routing properly
  • Read the Advanced Topics section of this site
  • Start reading Bogatin's "Signal and Power Integrity — Simplified"

Year 1+ · Serious designer

4-layer boards, high-speed signals, EMC

  • Design your first 4-layer board — the extra cost at JLCPCB is minimal, the learning is enormous
  • Tackle a DDR interface or gigabit Ethernet design
  • Learn LTspice for power supply and filter simulation
  • Study IPC-2221 (Generic Standard on Printed Board Design)
  • Consider open-sourcing your designs on GitHub — community feedback accelerates learning dramatically
